## Cooking Mechanics Overview

### Unlock Requirements
- Reach **Developer's Guild (DG) Level 5**
- Obtain a **Hobby Expansion Ticket** (automatic at required level)
- Speak to **Massimo** at his cafe in Town Center
- Receive a **free home stove** upon unlocking

### How Cooking Works
1. Approach a stove (home stove or Massimo's cafe)
2. Open recipe menu and select a learned recipe
3. Add required ingredients from inventory
4. Complete the **QTE (Quick Time Event)**: tap the spatula icon before the timer circle empties
5. Missing the QTE converts the dish to **"Bizarre Food"** or **"Bizarre Drink"** (sells for 30G, +10 energy)

### Cooking Stations
- **Simplistic Stove** - Starting stove (free on unlock)
- **Natural Stove** - Upgraded stove (bought from Massimo)
- **Industrial Stove** - Advanced stove (bought from Massimo)
- Craftable personal stoves at workbench (timber + ore)
- Recommended: **2-4 stoves** for efficient mass production
- Multiple cooking slots allow parallel production

### Experience & Leveling
- Each cooked dish awards XP toward cooking level
- **New recipes grant bonus XP** (more than repeated dishes)
- **Variants count as separate discoveries** for bonus XP
- 13 cooking levels total
- Collection certification tiers at: 10, 30, 60, 80, and all 98 recipe discoveries

## Star Quality System & Price Multipliers

### Confirmed Star Multipliers (from price data analysis)

Based on extensive cross-referencing of actual sell prices across all recipes, the multipliers are:

| Star Rating | Price Multiplier (vs 1-star) | Example: Apple Jam |
|-------------|-----------------------------|--------------------|
| 1 star      | 1.0x (base)                 | 270G               |
| 2 star      | 1.5x                        | 405G               |
| 3 star      | 2.0x                        | 540G               |
| 4 star      | 4.0x                        | 1,080G             |
| 5 star      | 8.0x                        | 2,160G             |

**These multipliers are confirmed consistent across ALL recipes.**

### What Determines Star Quality
Three factors influence the star rating of a cooked dish:
1. **Ingredient Quality** (biggest factor) - Higher-star ingredients produce higher-star dishes
2. **Cooking Skill Level** - Higher cooking level improves baseline quality
3. **Random Chance** - Some RNG element involved

### Energy Scaling by Star
Energy restoration also scales with star quality, though at different rates per recipe.

### Key Strategic Tips

1. **Cook, don't sell raw** - Cooking nearly always increases ingredient value
2. **Forage free ingredients** - Mushrooms, fruits from trees/bushes cost nothing
3. **Maximize star quality** - A 5-star dish sells 8x more than 1-star
4. **Use multiple stoves** - 2-4 stoves recommended for mass production
5. **Low-value fish -> recipes** - Cook fish worth <100G; sell fish worth >200G raw
6. **Use 1-star ingredients for better profit margins** (lower cost, decent output)
7. **Fertilize crops** to improve crop star quality, which improves dish quality
8. **Level cooking hobby** to improve baseline dish quality

### HIGH CONFIDENCE (verified across 3+ sources):
- All Level 1-9 recipe names, ingredients, and sell prices
- Star multiplier formula: 1x / 1.5x / 2x / 4x / 8x
- Crop data (Levels 1-8)
- Foraging locations and free ingredient list
- Cooking unlock requirements and progression

### MEDIUM CONFIDENCE (2 sources or partially verified):
- Shop ingredient prices (Milk 50G, Eggs 100G, Cheese 100G, Butter 150G)
- "Cost to Make" values
- Energy restoration values
- Recipe purchase costs
- XP requirements per level

### LOW CONFIDENCE (single source or TBD):
- Level 10-13 recipe details (community has not fully documented)
- Some ingredient shop prices (Flour, Sugar, Coffee Beans, Cooking Oil)
- Crop data for Tea Tree, Cacao, Avocado
- Exact Doris item prices
- Some buff/special effect details

### CONFLICTING DATA NOTES:
- **Recipe count**: heartopia-tips.com says 64 known recipes; heartopia.gg says 98 total; heartopia.life says 77. The 98 number likely includes ALL variants (each jam flavor, each mushroom pie variant, each roll cake color). The 64 number is confirmed documented recipes with complete data.
- **Fish N Chips ingredients**: Some sources say "2x Fish, 2x Potato", others say "1x Fish, 2x Potato". The heartopia-tips.com wiki (most detailed) does not clarify quantity precisely.
- **House Salad ingredients**: heartopia.cc says "2x Tomato, 1x Lettuce" while heartopia.life says "2x Any Vegetable". This may reflect a game update or variant system.
- **Crop star multipliers** appear different from recipe star multipliers (~1.33x per star for crops vs the 1.5x/2x/4x/8x pattern for recipes).
